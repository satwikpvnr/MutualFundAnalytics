\# EDA Findings



\## Chart 1: NAV Trend Analysis



\- SBI Small Cap Direct Growth (AMFI 119598) showed the strongest NAV growth from 2022 to 2026.

\- NAV increased from approximately 90 to over 320 during the analysis period.

\- SBI Bluechip funds demonstrated stable long-term growth with lower volatility.

\- SBI Magnum Gilt Fund showed the least volatility and steady returns.

\- Small-cap funds outperformed bluechip and gilt funds in terms of NAV appreciation.

\- Market corrections were visible during 2024–2025, followed by recovery and growth.

## Chart 2: AUM Growth by AMC



\- SBI Mutual Fund maintained the highest AUM across the period.

\- ICICI Prudential and HDFC Mutual Fund remained among the top asset managers.

\- Most AMCs showed steady AUM growth from 2022 to 2025.

\- Industry-wide AUM expanded significantly, indicating increasing investor participation.

\- Large AMCs dominated market share compared to smaller players.

## Chart 3: SIP Inflow Trend



\- SIP inflows increased from ₹11,517 crore in January 2022 to ₹31,002 crore in December 2025.

\- Retail investor participation grew steadily over the four-year period.

\- The strongest growth phase occurred during 2024–2025.

\- SIP collections crossed the ₹30,000 crore milestone in late 2025.

\- The trend indicates increasing investor confidence in long-term wealth creation through mutual funds.



\## Chart 4: Category-wise Inflow Heatmap



\- Liquid Funds recorded the highest net inflows across all periods.

\- Sectoral/Thematic funds attracted strong investor interest.

\- Large \& Mid Cap, Flexi Cap, and Mid Cap categories maintained consistent inflows.

\- ELSS and Gilt funds showed relatively lower inflow volumes.

\- Overall category inflows remained positive, indicating healthy market participation.



\## Chart 5: Investor Demographics



\- The 26–35 age group is the largest investor segment.

\- The 36–45 age group is the second-largest contributor.

\- Young investors (18–25) are actively participating in mutual fund investments.

\- Investors aged 56+ represent the smallest investor group.

\- Average investment amounts remain relatively consistent across all age groups.



\## Chart 6: Geographic Distribution



\- Punjab recorded the highest investor transaction count.

\- Madhya Pradesh and Tamil Nadu were among the top participating states.

\- Investor participation is geographically diverse across India.

\- T30 cities contributed 66.3% of total transactions.

\- B30 cities contributed 33.7%, indicating strong mutual fund adoption beyond major metropolitan regions.



\## Chart 7: Folio Growth Analysis



\- Total mutual fund folios increased from 13.26 crore (Jan 2022) to 26.12 crore (Jan 2026).

\- The industry added approximately 12.86 crore new folios during the four-year period.

\- Folio count grew by nearly 97%, indicating rapid expansion of the investor base.

\- Growth accelerated significantly during 2024–2025, with folios rising sharply across all fund categories.

\- The industry crossed the 20 crore folio milestone in 2024, reflecting increasing retail participation.

\- Continuous upward growth with no major declines highlights strong investor confidence in mutual funds.

\- Rising SIP adoption and greater financial awareness contributed significantly to folio growth.

\- January 2026 recorded the highest folio count at 26.12 crore, marking a new industry peak.



\## Chart 8: Correlation Matrix



\- Most mutual fund schemes show weak correlations, with values close to zero.

\- Correlation coefficients range approximately between -0.07 and 0.05.

\- No strong positive or negative relationships were observed among the selected schemes.

\- The low correlation indicates that fund performance varies independently across categories.

\- Diversification benefits can be achieved by investing across multiple schemes.

\- The absence of highly correlated funds reduces concentration risk in a portfolio.

\- Small-cap, large-cap, hybrid, and debt-oriented schemes exhibit different return patterns.

\- Overall, the mutual fund portfolio demonstrates good diversification characteristics.



\## Chart 9: Sector Allocation



\- Banking sector held the largest portfolio allocation at 22.0% of total holdings.

\- Information Technology (15.3%) and Pharma (13.7%) were the next largest sectors.

\- Automobile sector contributed 10.9%, indicating significant exposure to the manufacturing industry.

\- Utilities (8.9%) and FMCG (7.7%) provided defensive sector diversification.

\- Infrastructure (6.5%) and Telecom (4.9%) represented emerging growth opportunities.

\- Consumer Goods accounted for 4.3% of the portfolio allocation.

\- Portfolio exposure is well distributed across multiple sectors, reducing concentration risk.

\- The combination of Banking, IT, Pharma, and FMCG reflects a balanced mix of growth and defensive investments.
